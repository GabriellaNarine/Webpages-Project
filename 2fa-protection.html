Advanced Protection: Two-Factor Authentication

Even strong passwords can be vulnerable. Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) adds another layer of security to your accounts.

2FA requires not only a password but also a second verification method, like a code sent to your phone or email.

According to the FTC, even if a hacker steals your password, they won't be able to access your account without the second authentication factor.

Using 2FA protects you against phishing attacks, malware, and brute-force attacks, making it an essential part of your online security strategy.
